Net Promoter Score (NPS)

Web App

The NPS is widely used by businesses to measure how likely customers are to recommend a company, product, or service to others.

Customers get asked a straightforward question: "How likely are you to recommend our product/service to a friend or colleague?" They respond on a scale of 0 to 10, where 0 means "not likely" and 10 means "extremely likely."

Based on their responses, customers fall into three categories: Promoters (score 9-10), Passives (score 7-8), and Detractors (score 0-6).

These scores help us understand how our customers perceive our products and services and guides us in providing the best possible experience.

THE PROBLEM

NPS Implementation Challenges in Food Tech

Various industries, including aviation, banking, hospitality, tourism, market research, restaurants, retail, spa, and healthcare, commonly use NPS (Net Promoter Score). Adapting the NPS survey for RockSpoon, a food technology company specializing in POS and management solutions for restaurants, posed a unique challenge.

APPROACH

Exploring NPS Implementation and Competitive Insights

I began by looking into the established norms for implementing an NPS survey and delving into our competitors' strategies. I wanted to understand what they were doingâ€”whether they had an NPS system in place. After thorough research, here are my key findings:

Send NPS surveys both in-app and via email To gauge immediate user sentiment and understand customer support interactions and tool usage.
Use NPS at multiple stages of the user journey During onboarding, when users have reached an activation milestone, after adoption, and on unique date-set.
Conduct NPS survey frequently For gaining valuable insights.
Send NPS surveys to the right amount of customers The sample size is important when conducting a bulk survey, you generally want to target a moderate size to be safe.
Always include a follow-up qualitative question Acknowledge feedback received from customers, and learn more from your promoters and drive word-of-mouth.

SOLUTION

Simplicity for Survey Success

I came to recognize that for a survey to achieve maximum effectiveness, it should be clear, concise, and easily comprehensible for customers. It is crucial to steer clear of industry jargon or technical terms that may lead to confusion. To attain these objectives, the survey should incorporate the following features:

  • Customizable Survey Question.

  • Real-time feedback.

  • Segmentation Capabilities.

  • Integration with CRM tools.

  • Cultural Barriers.

  • Multilingual Support.
