To restaurateurs, RockSpoon has long been known as a one home SaaS platform to manage every aspect of their needs and daily operations.

Despite our online presence, our designers grappled with maintaining consistency and reliability in feature updates, while developers struggled to translate designs into reality for a total of 4 applications: Fixed POS, Mobile POS, Payment Tablet, and KDS (Kitchen Display Screen).

This prompted us to start the development of our first design system, and I took the lead, collaborating with our engineers to meticulously craft a robust design system from the ground up.

Since all our devices run android OS, we had to follow Google Material Design System guidelines, which also provided us with all their components. But if you truly want to maximize the potential of the native Android UI then you have to build it yourself, Google doesn’t give you everything.

APPROACH

Atomic Design System

I made my choice to follow the Atomic Design System methodology. This approach breaks UI elements into reusable components (atoms, molecules, organisms, templates, and pages) for a scalable and consistent design.

Then, since our theme layer and the majority of the elements have already been established, I audited the products and searched for patterns that could help me define a style guide, and create components from.

STYLE GUIDE

But First: Tokens!

You don't start building a house without it's foundation, the same thing applies to design systems.

Design tokens are the fundamental building blocks of all UI elements, they point to styles and values like:

Grid System

I went with a 4-point grid system, which was the secret sauce for design harmony, easily aligning elements on the canvas.

Color Styles

Our devs asked me to have 2 color styles: Reference, the source of truth colors & System, the ones related to system settings, like “light” & “Dark” themes. Color Styles should also include state-layers and surface colors, which act as a key factor in defining component interaction & animation.

Text Styles

The default typeface for RockSpoon isRoboto.
I created our text styles while carefully choosing sizes, heights, and weights based on the business needs.

Once Tokens are defined, both designers and engineers can be confident that the same values will be used in their mockups and implementation!

Developing RockSpoon Design system presented 2 key challenges: scalability & adaptability. With a diverse range of devices, each with distinct characteristics, we needed to ensure that every component remained responsive across small screens (mobile), medium-sized screens (tablet), and in both portrait and landscape orientations, while also accommodating light and dark modes.

State layers are the frames that can hold a specific color, outline, or even opacity, to present a specific interaction with the main component, i.e buttons.

These kind of layers can be super helpful and, if integrated correctly, can help you save a lot of time and rework.

For example, if you were tasked to change the main brand color, instead of going through each comp and state layer, you can simply change the color values that you have already defined and simply watch the magic happens!

Buttons were designed to be either high or medium emphasis for important or common actions.

The primary buttons are high emphasis, and the main actions are to search, create, or compose.

The secondary buttons are medium emphasis, and the main actions are to not distract users from other onscreen elements.

Other Atoms

Alerts

An alert component is a user-friendly element designed to convey important messages like warnings, errors, successes, and information. However, the main challenge with such instances is to make them adaptable to any text length and number of rows.

Search & Text Fields

This one was tricky, because search bars are basically text-fields. So I had to incorporate the text field inside the search component while making sure that it does provide all the necessary properties/states that might be needed.

Checkboxes & Radio Buttons

We all know what are these for: single choices from a list? go with radio buttons, otherwise, checkboxes are the alternative.

However, the hard part, in building such components, is to define all the states, and to maintain the same ratio while resizing.

There's a lot of atoms to explore, including Counters, FABs, Items, Loaders, Toggles, Tabs and so much more! Just like atoms in the natural world, each interface atom has unique properties, like hero image dimensions or primary heading font size. These properties guide their usage in the broader UI system. And always remember, interface atoms truly shine when put into action.

In a nutshell...

A Design System is the heartbeat of thoughtful product design, enabling Designers & Developers to construct scalable, yet adaptable products. I’ve found out that it acts like a dependable product within a company because building it requires:

1

Discovery

Understanding the already established styles and guidelines while conducting detailed researches about best practices.

2

Discovery

Crafting tokens & components, while taking into consideration reusability and scalability.

3

Discovery

Every single crafted style/comp should be aligned with the devs and tested by the designers while building their screens, this will provoke a cycle of component optimization, and variations addition, hence design system evolvement!

4

Discovery

Continuous monitoring, updates, user feedback analysis, and iterative improvements to enhance the system’s features and user experience.

5

Discovery

Ongoing support, bug fixes, and feature enhancements to keep the system running smoothly.
